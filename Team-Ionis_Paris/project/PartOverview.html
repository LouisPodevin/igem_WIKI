Proposed implementation - IGEM IONIS

Part overview

To insert our genes of interest in our bacteria, we worked with three different plasmids: pSB1A3 (high copy number), pSB4A5 (low copy number) and pSB4K5 (low copy number).
They contain the resistance gene of ampicillin and kanamycin, respectively.

All BioBricks are inserted between the prefix and suffix region of the mentioned plasmids according to the BioBrick RFC[10] assembly standards.

Figure 2: Our constructs for the expression and localization of the T4 bacteriophage gp37 to the outer membrane of our chassis

The BioBrick BBa_K3409008 is coding for the expression of the gene product 37 of then T4 bacteriophage LTF. Thanks to the co-expressed Lpp-OmpA genes, the gp37 will be directed on the external surface of Escherichia coli. A His-Tag and a RFP have been added to validate the BioBrick.
Additionally, to ensure the proper folding of the gp37, our BioBrick includes the coding sequence for two chaperone proteins gp38 and gp57a.

The BioBrick BBa_K3409010 contains the gene mcpM which when expressed, will lead microcin PDI. This microcin has two N-Ter signal peptides which are cleaved when secreted.

The gene mcpA also present in this BioBrick encodes for an activator of McpM and contains a CaaX protease domain. Finally, the gene mcpI encodes for the immunity protein of McpM for the producing strain.

Figure 4: Our constructs for the expression and secretion of McpM

The BioBrick BBa_K3409011 is coding for the McpD protein which acts as a membrane fusion protein creating a channel through the periplasm and connects to TolC.

The BioBrick BBa_K3409012 is coding for the McpB protein which contains transmembrane domains as well as nucleotide binding domains which have highly conserved motifs characteristic of the ABC transporter superfamily of proteins.

The BioBrick BBa_K3519012 contains in its sequence the gene coding for AraC (BBa_K2442103). This regulatory protein acts differently in function of the absence or presence of L-Arabinose.

In absence of arabinose, it binds to two specific half-sites found in the minimal pBAD promoter (BBa_K2442101), inducing a DNA loop that excludes the RNA polymerase from binding to pBAD. This prevents the expression of DNASE I (BBa_K3519004), a gene whose product is an endonuclease cleaving DNA.

On the contrary, when L-Arabinose is present, it binds to AraC to induce conformational changes. AraC will then bind to other half-sites, giving access for RNA polymerase to bind pBAD. This leads to the production of the endonuclease bpDNaseI, leading to the destruction of our bacteria and preventing its dissemination in the environment.

Figure 6: The IISER TIRUPATI Team constructs for the expression and secretion of DNASE I

The BioBrick BBa_K3519011 also contains in its sequence the gene coding for AraC (BBa_K2442103).

Thus, in absence of arabinose, the DNA loop will exclude the RNA polymerase from binding to pBAD, preventing the expression of mRFP (BBa_E1010), a gene whose product is the Red FLuorescent Protein.

On the contrary, when L-Arabinose is present, RNA polymerase will have a free-access to pBAD, leading to RFP production.

By replacing DNASE I by mRFP, the expression of the gene following pBAD can be easily detected through fluorescence assays. Furthermore, its quantification will enable the validation of our construct.
