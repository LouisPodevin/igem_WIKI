Fundraising department

Giulia Criseo for her dedication in finding sponsors, mainly laboratory tools needed for our proof of concept. This year was a little special because of Covid-19, but she still managed to find sponsors and ways to finance our project and laboratory experiments. She also created a crowdfunding pot on kisskissbankbank.com in order to collect funds from friends and family.

Communication department

Giulia Criseo and Albane Mabro for the communication of our project on all of our social media (Facebook, Instagram, Linkedin and Twitter). They also organized the iGEMeetParis international virtual meetup in collaboration with 4 other Parisian iGEM teams; GO Paris Saclay, Evry, Sorbonne and Paris Bettencourt.

Finally, they participated in challenges and collaborations from other iGEM teams, such as “Paper plane project” and “Postcard project”.

Human Practices department

Renaud Eynard and Thomas Kobak for planning several events such as the presentations of our project in secondary schools and in science summer camps. These events include participating in the “Fête de la Science”, which is a French event where people can present their project, company, studies or job to the public.

Thomas also interviewed several farmers in order to collect their opinion and insight on antimicrobial resistance.

Software department

David Padrino and Thomas Kobak for their skills in developing our project's software, which from a target pathogenic bacterium, is able to design the key plasmid to be integrated into our study model.

IT/Wiki department
