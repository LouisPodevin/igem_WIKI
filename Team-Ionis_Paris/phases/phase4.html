Galenic form

The gastrointestinal (GI) tract represents a crucial interface between the host and the environment. As such, it has the double role of rejecting pathogens while simplifying nutrient absorption. Approximatively 1014 microbes colonize it with a continuous increasing colonization density from the stomach to the end of the colon1.

For the most part, bacteria in the human gut are not dangerous. In fact, several health benefits are linked to the presence of appropriate gut bacteria, such as better digestion, weight loss, improved immune function and a lower risk of many diseases. Furthermore, the consumption of probiotics brings beneficial properties for human gut health2.

Probiotics are living micro-organisms which, when administered in adequate amounts confer a health benefit for the host3. Some yeasts function as probiotics but, most of the time, they are bacteria. Probiotics should not be mistaken for prebiotics, which consist in non-digestible food ingredients that benefit the host by enhancing the activity of one or a limited number of beneficial bacteria4. The benefits brought by probiotics rely on several mechanisms, including the reduction of invasion and colonization by pathogens, decreasing intestinal pH, changing the host’s immune response, etc5.

For a product to be qualified as a probiotic, it must be in conformity with several conditions1:
Producing antimicrobial resitance
Identified at the genus and strain level
Able to survive intestinal transit

Able to survive intestinal transit

When orally taken, food enters through the oral cavity to continue through the pharynx, the esophagus, the stomach, the small intestine, the large intestine and finally it exits through the anus6. As the key parameter for an optimal therapeutic effect is the viability up to the enteral site, probiotics must remain intact through the upper intestinal tract7.

Hence, the choice of the galenic form is crucial.

For probiotics, two main systems enable its delayed delivery: pharmaceutical formulations and food-based products. Pharmaceutical preparations are considered more effective compared to commercial food-based carrier systems although their preference mainly depends on consumers’ perception8. These pharmaceuticals can be found under the galenic form of liquids, tablets, power beads and capsules9.

Nowadays, encapsulation is the most explored method for improving probiotic survival and the delivery of bioactive compounds. It enables the protection of the inner active materials from gastric juice thanks to specific capsules which are resistant to acidic environments and at the same time, allow a delayed release into the intestine through their sensitivity to alkaline environments10. This information, associated to the fact that 42% of probiotic consumers prefer hard-shell capsules rather than any other galenic form, suggests that hard-shell capsules are the best solution7.

The life-time of hard-shell capsules is also an important parameter to consider. For hard-shell encapsulated probiotics, capsules are totally disintegrated after approximatively 1 hour in gastric acids11. But stomach emptying rate varies with the chemical and the physical composition of the meal12. As the emptying time of a solid meal in the stomach is about 2 hours, a probiotic would be delivered to the stomach in this condition, exposing it to gastric acids which would destroy it. The amount of time being about 20 minutes for a liquid meal, this condition would allow delivery to the enteral site13.

Thus, to enable the arrival of our therapeutic bacteria into the gut, it should be orally taken in the morning, with water if needed, at least 20 minutes prior to the first meal.

If needed, we could have included a BioBrick providing gastric acid resistance in our chassis (this has been done by the 2019 Fudan team)14. However, considering the previously presented information, the addition of this kind of ability seems unnecessary in the context of BacTail.

While hard shell capsules have traditionally been made using animal-based gelatin (beef bones, pork skin, fish…), many manufacturers have begun to seek out plant-based alternatives to meet evolving consumer needs. As the largest possible market is targeted, the possibility to use plant-based capsules has been studied and they present a similar resistance to gastric acids than regular capsules. Manufacturers looking for a vegan-friendly solution to hard shell capsules have several options ranging from a variety of seaweed-based alternatives, to modified starch 15.

Identified at the genus and strain level

E. coli Nissle 1917 (EcN) is a probiotic strain largely studied worldwide16. Firstly described in 1917 as presenting antagonistic effects toward intestinal pathogens, it remains today the active agent of Mutaflor®, a cure for numerous gastrointestinal diseases17. By determining its complete genomic DNA sequence of 5,023-Mbp in 2013 18, several studies were conducted to identify its gene products (Fig. 1). Results claimed that this nonpathogenic strain presents an intestinal anti-inflammatory effect without major immunotoxic properties nor virulence factors 20, 21, 22. Furthermore, the safety, tolerability, and efficacy of consuming EcN was investigated with encouraging conclusions.

These parameters, associated with its innocuous nature and its flexibility for bioengineering17, makes this bacterium an ideal chassis candidate for our project. Due to a lack of time we were not able to investigate the feasibility of transforming this strain with our constructs. However, this chassis would not only have had specifically targeted antibiotic resistant bacteria, but it would have also brought the same advantageous effects to the patients previously described while reassuring skeptical consumers.
